# UK Road Traffic Accidents: Exploring Casualty Patterns in 2014

## Project Overview 
This project presents an interactive Tableau dashboard exploring road traffic casualty patterns in the UK during 2014 using a publicly available dataset from Kaggle containing over 1.05 million road traffic records collected between 2005 and 2014.

The objective of the dashboard is to identify when casualties were most common, examine how casualty severity varied throughout the day, and explore where the highest concentrations of casualties occurred.

## Dashboard Features:

### Casualties By Month 
Displays the total number of casualties recorded each month during 2014.

Key insight: October recorded the highest number of casualties, with 17,717 casualties.

### Casualties By Day Of The Week 
Shows the total number of casualties for each day of the week during October.

Key insight: Saturday recorded the highest number of casualties (3,215), making it the focus of the remaining analysis.

### Saturday Casualties By Hour 
A bar chart illustrating how casualty numbers change throughout the day.

Key insight: Casualties peaked around 08:00 and 15:00

### Severity Distribution at Peak Hours (08:00 & 15:00, Saturdays in October)
A pie chart summarising the proportion of casualties across severity levels during the two busiest hours (08:00 and 15:00).

Key insight: Severity 3 casualties represented the largest share of casualties.

### Severity 3 Casualty Hotspots (08:00 & 15:00, Saturdays in October)
A density map showing the geographic distribution of Severity 3 casualties occurring at 08:00 and 15:00 on Saturdays during October.

Key insight: The highest concentration of casualties is observed around the Greater London area.

### Casualties by Road Type and Severity With No Special Conditions At Site
A stacked bar chart showing Saturday casualty counts in October across different road types where no special road conditions were recorded.

Key insight: Single carriageways recorded the highest number of casualties across all severity levels.

## Key Findings 
The analysis identified several notable patterns in the 2014 data:
- October recorded the highest number of casualties.
- Saturdays experienced more casualties than any other day of the week during October.
- Casualty numbers peaked at approximately 08:00 and 15:00.
- Severity 3 (slight) casualties accounted for the majority of recorded casualties.
- The highest concentration of Severity 3 casualties occurred around Greater London.
- Single carriageways accounted for the greatest number of recorded casualties across all severity categories.
